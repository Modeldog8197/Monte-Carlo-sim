THE MATH
Central Limit Theorem
X̄ₙ → N(μ, σ²/n) as n→∞

The mean of N samples converges to a normal distribution regardless of the underlying distribution — the foundation of why Monte Carlo works.

Standard Error
SE = σ / √N

Simulation error shrinks as 1/√N. Doubling accuracy requires 4× the trials. 10,000 trials gives ~1% standard error on most estimates.

Geometric Brownian Motion
dS = μS dt + σS dW

The SDE governing asset prices under Black-Scholes. μ is drift, σ is volatility, dW is a Wiener process increment. Discretised for simulation.

Box-Muller Transform
Z = √(-2 ln U₁) · cos(2π U₂)

Converts uniform random numbers to standard normal variates. The workhorse behind every normal distribution sample in this engine.
